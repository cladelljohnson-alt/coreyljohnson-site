7 Must-Read YA Mysteries Featuring Black Protagonists

The YA mystery genre has always rewarded readers who want more than plot. The best books in this space use the mystery as a frame for something deeper: questions about justice, about who gets believed, about what it costs to keep looking for the truth when everyone around you has moved on.

These seven books center Black protagonists who refuse to let the mystery go, and each one earns its place on this list.

Allegedly by Tiffany D. Jackson. A teenager maintains her innocence in a crime she may or may not have committed. Jackson does not make it easy to be comfortable. The tension never lets up.

Dear Martin by Nic Stone. Not a traditional mystery, but built around one of the most urgent questions in America: what happens to a Black teenager when the system designed to protect him becomes the threat? Stone writes with precision and fury.

All American Boys by Jason Reynolds and Brendan Kiely. Two perspectives, one incident, and a story that forces the reader to hold both truths at once. The mystery here is not whodunit. It is what we do with what we know.

Monday's Not Coming by Tiffany D. Jackson. A girl goes missing and no one seems to notice. The reader notices everything. This is Jackson at her most unsettling.

The Poet X by Elizabeth Acevedo. A young woman finding her voice in a world that keeps trying to define her. The mystery is internal, but no less urgent for it.

Ghost Squad by Claribel Ortega. For younger YA readers, this one blends mystery with cultural identity and does both well.

They Never Came Home by Corey L. Johnson. A missing girl. A community that cannot agree on what happened. And teenagers who will not stop until they know the truth. This is the tradition these books share, and it is one worth reading all the way through.

Each of these stories trusts its Black protagonist with the hardest questions. That trust is what makes them worth your time.

Closing Thoughts

These stories remind us that suspense and representation go hand in hand. For readers craving gripping mysteries with Black protagonists, there's never been a better time to explore.
